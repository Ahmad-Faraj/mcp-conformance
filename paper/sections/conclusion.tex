The MCP server ecosystem has been measured statically many times but never
executed at scale. By installing and speaking the protocol to a complete census of
the eligible registry, we found that a large fraction of installable servers do not
successfully start, and that among those that do, a near-universal divergence from
the written specification's error-handling categorization is inherited directly
from the official SDKs. Neither fact is visible to a static scanner or a registry
crawl; both matter to anyone building on this layer. We release our conformance
harness and dataset so that the measurement can be repeated, extended to remote and
credentialed servers, and---most usefully---run by server authors before they
publish. Conformance, not just security, is now a measurable and improvable property
of the ecosystem.
